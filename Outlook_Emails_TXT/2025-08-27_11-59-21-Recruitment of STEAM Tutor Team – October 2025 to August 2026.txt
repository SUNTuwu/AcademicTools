Dear all Engineering UG students, 

 

Recruitment of STEAM Tutor Team – October 2025 to August 2026

 

 

 

Center for Global & Community Engagement (GCE) is looking for outgoing students to work with us in STEAM Tutor Team for various STEAM activities from October 2025 to August 2026.

 

STEAM tutors who chose to join the Micro:bit sub-team will help in delivering STEAM Workshop Development to school students and general public, and Underwater Robotics with Arduino sub-team will help in development of community engagement programmes related to Underwater Robotics . 2 subteams will also work together in different STEAM programmes.

 

STEAM Tutors can gain exposure and teaching experience in STEAM workshops and meet youths from various backgrounds.

  

Below is the general timeline of application and initial training for your consideration:

 

Mon, 15 Sept 2025, 2359 : 

Application Closed

Thur, 18 Sept 2025, 1800 – 2130 :                 

Face to Face, Group Interview (Compulsory) (30-min per session)

 

Mon, 22 Sept 2025, 1800 – 2100 :                 

Welcome and Training Session (Compulsory) 

Mon, 29 Sept 2025 or Thur, 2 Oct 2025  1800 – 2100 :  

Technical Skill Training for respective stream

 

 

Eligibilities

1.	Students from all disciplines are welcome to apply. 
2.	Proactive in learning basic STEAM knowledge, teaching and project development.
3.	Previous experience in helping in STEAM activities (e.g. STEAM Day@HKUST ) and teaching children science and engineering are advantages. 

4.	Available to work on weekends. 
5.	Good command of Cantonese, English, and/or Putonghua.
6.	Shortlisted candidates will be invited to attend an interview on Thur, 18 Sept 2025. 

7.	Must attend the training session in the evenings of Mon, 22 Sept 2025, Mon, 29 Sept 2025 and Thur, 2 Oct 2025.

  

 

Job Duties

1.	To develop and prepare materials for teachings and demonstrations of STEAM projects.

2.	To teach participants how to assemble the products and STEM knowledge in the workshops.
3.	To mingle with the participants and introduce them the study life at HKUST.
4.	To perform other ad-hoc tasks and provide logistical support to the workshops.
